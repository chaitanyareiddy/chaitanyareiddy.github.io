01

Context

Regulatory teams operate in environments where each answer may be revisited by auditors, inspectors, and review boards months or years later. Decisions must be traceable, explainable, and defensible.

Generic AI tools optimize for conversational speed. Regulated teams optimize for verified evidence chains, scoping, and governance. The real bottleneck is not intelligence — it is time-to-verified-answer.

The Core Tension
Generic AI optimizes for Speed · Fluency
Regulated teams optimize for Verification · Traceability
Real bottleneck Time-to-Verified-Answer
02

Problem Quantification

Through discovery with regulatory affairs and safety teams, I modeled the current workflow for answering submission, labeling, and guidance-related questions.

  • Average document retrieval time per query: 18–25 minutes
  • Each answer requires 3–5 documents cross-referenced
  • 12–15% of outputs require rework due to unclear citations
  • Heavy SME dependency creates coordination bottlenecks

Modeled assumptions grounded in discovery interviews and workflow shadowing — not marketing estimates.

03

Hypothesis

If we redesign regulatory search around time-to-verified-answer, we can materially improve productivity and governance without sacrificing control.

Interventions
  • Citation-grounded RAG with line-level references
  • Confidence scoring on each answer
  • Scoped document search by jurisdiction and product
  • Human-in-the-loop validation and override workflow
Expected Outcomes
Time-to-Verified-Answer ↓ ≥30%
Citation rework rate ↓ Materially
SME dependency ↓ Edge cases only
User trust + adoption ↑ Via evidence visibility

MVP Data Validation Strategy

The MVP was designed to validate or falsify three core assumptions before scaling to broader workflows and integrations.

Assumption 01
Citation visibility increases trust
Mechanism
Inline citations with deep links into source documents
Measurement
Citation click-through rate + verified answers with opened citations
Validation Signal
Higher verification when citations inspected = trust driver confirmed
Assumption 02
Scoped retrieval improves precision
Mechanism
User- and admin-defined scoped collections by jurisdiction and product
Measurement
Answer acceptance rate — scoped vs. global search
Validation Signal
Uplift in acceptance validates scoping investment
Assumption 03
Confidence scoring reduces risk
Mechanism
Confidence bands tied to citation density, recency, and source type
Measurement
Human override frequency across confidence bands
Validation Signal
Low-confidence answers overridden more = confidence is a risk triage tool
06

Experimentation Plan

Phase 1
Document Upload + RAG Retrieval

Prove that teams can ingest their corpus and get cited answers that outperform existing search tools.

  • Core flows for repository connection and upload
  • Scoped collection creation per product and jurisdiction
  • Measured TVA, acceptance rate, and retention over 2–4 weeks
Phase 2
Confidence Scoring + A/B Test

Measure how exposing confidence affects trust, override behavior, and adoption.

Variant A
Confidence scores + explanations visible
Variant B
Confidence hidden; citations and scopes intact

Measured: trust perception via micro-surveys, override rate, adoption lift, TVA delta

Trade-offs & Governance

Designing regulated AI requires explicit trade-offs rather than chasing generic intelligence. Every constraint is a deliberate governance decision.

Constrained answers
vs. Flexible generation

Answers are limited to cited, in-scope documents. Less creative — but outputs stay within the validated corpus and pass audit scrutiny.

Auditability
vs. Conversational freedom

Interactions are structured and logged, not ephemeral chat. Every decision can be reconstructed and reviewed months later.

Trust over speed
vs. Raw throughput

Verification checkpoints add friction intentionally. Deliberate checks reduce downstream rework and compliance risk at submission time.

09

Reflection

In regulated AI, optimization is not the goal. Accountability is.

The strongest AI systems are not the most expressive. They are the most traceable.

Krisper is a deliberate exercise in treating AI as infrastructure for accountable decision-making. Every design decision — from scoped retrieval to confidence scoring to human overrides — exists to make the system trustworthy in the moments that matter most.
